### Classification of Research Topics in Neural Aerial and Satellite Reconstruction

#### 1. True Digital Orthophoto Map (TDOM) Generation
This topic focuses on leveraging explicit and implicit neural rendering techniques to generate distortion-free, high-precision orthophotos from UAV and satellite imagery. Traditional TDOM generation often suffers from jagged edges, ghosting, and complex visibility analysis requirements. To overcome this, novel frameworks like Ortho-NeRF employ orthogonal ray sampling and volume rendering to eliminate perspective deformations. Similarly, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) adaptations, such as Ortho-3DGS and HyGS-TDOM, utilize depth-regulated optimization and parallel orthogonal splatting to achieve commercial-grade geometric accuracy and real-time generation speeds, completely bypassing traditional occlusion detection flaws. 

#### 2. Large-Scale Urban and Cultural Heritage Reconstruction
Research in this category evaluates the scalability and geometric fidelity of NeRF and 3DGS when applied to massive cityscapes and intricate heritage objects. Because standard neural rendering struggles with GPU memory limits in large environments, studies focus on semantic-aware grouping, divide-and-conquer chunking strategies (e.g., PG-SAG), and progressive partitioning to distribute computational loads. Additionally, this topic includes comparative analyses between standard Structure-from-Motion (SfM) photogrammetry and neural methods for the digital preservation of heritage buildings and ethnographic museum collections, emphasizing 3DGS's superior capability in recovering fine-grained details and textures efficiently.

#### 3. Shadow Modeling and Illumination Adaptation
A significant challenge in multi-date satellite photogrammetry is the appearance inconsistency caused by shifting shadows, transient objects, and varying solar elevation angles. Research under this topic explicitly models lighting and environmental dynamics to decouple scene geometry from transient illumination. Frameworks like Shadow Neural Radiance Fields (S-NeRF) and SatGS integrate solar direction vectors to predict local solar visibility and ambient sky color, enabling shadow-free novel view synthesis. Furthermore, the introduction of large-scale datasets like S-EO provides essential ground-truth geometry and shadow annotations to advance geometry-aware shadow detection and 3D reconstruction in Earth observation.

#### 4. Few-Shot and Sparse-View Aerial Synthesis
This research area addresses the severe geometry degradation and overfitting issues that occur when neural rendering models are trained on highly limited or sparse viewpoints—a common constraint at the boundaries of UAV flight paths. To stabilize training and enforce geometric constraints without dense image coverage, researchers develop progressive learning paradigms and uncertainty-guided models. Approaches utilizing Few-Shot Gaussian Splatting (FSGS) also integrate proximity-guided unpooling and monocular depth supervision to robustly reconstruct aerial scenes from as few as 2 to 5 views.

#### 5. 3D Change Detection and Dynamic Scene Analysis
This topic explores the extension of 3D and 4D neural representations to detect and map structural changes across multi-temporal sequences. Traditional change detection methods struggle with significant lighting variations and require strictly controlled conditions. New frameworks, such as "Gaussian Difference", embed pre- and post-change image sequences into 4D Gaussian Splatting representations. By rendering coherent image sequences and comparing segmented instance identifiers, these models can efficiently generate high-quality change maps from any viewing direction, overcoming the limitations of heavy computational costs and scene illumination variances.
